Executive Summary
What NostalPic is
A mobile app that automatically prints and ships your daily photos with no manual selection required.
A modern re‑imagining of disposable cameras: shoot → do nothing → prints arrive.
Optional AI filters out blur, duplicates, and bad frames.
Pain point solved
Parents and grandparents want prints but to avoid the work of organizing and selecting photos.
Most special events, such as weddings, pictures are taken, and either never shared or lost for printing purposes, NostalPic allows for ease of sharing & access to prints. 
Digital memories accumulate endlessly but are rarely enjoyed through the nostalgic experience of sifting through photo boxes together or enjoying physical photo albums. 
Option to pull in photos from IOS or Android camera app. 
Save storage from photo apps, as meaningful photos will be printed automatically. 
Why it wins
Zero‑friction workflow (camera‑first, auto‑print).
Emotional resonance: nostalgia, physical keepsakes, family connection.
Sharable collections across accounts.
Predictable pricing and simple batching (daily/weekly/monthly).
Strong upsell paths: photobooks, finishes, gifts.
Market
U.S. TAM > 100M adults (parents, Millennials→Boomers, ~70M grandparents).
Global photo printing & merchandise: $40–50B today, heading to $60–80B.
Emotional value substantiated by studies: printed photos strengthen bonding, identity, and well‑being.
Business model
Subscriptions (Basic/Plus/Family), royalties on print‑lab GMV, light ads.
Subscriptions drive the majority of revenue; royalties scale with volume.
Estimated Financial outlook (3‑year SOM) 
Year 1 revenue: ~$13.8M
Year 2 revenue: ~$41.5M
Year 3 revenue: ~$69.2M
Subscriptions represent ~82% of revenue; royalty ~17%.
Why now
Rising nostalgia trends.
Tech‑fatigue around curation and screen time.
Aging population that values physical prints.
Competitive advantage
No platform offers a camera‑first automatic printing pipeline, with optional AI filtering. 
Automated printed photos, photobooks and grandparent auto‑ship establish defensible differentiation.

9) Technology & Architecture
Mobile: iOS (Swift), Android (Kotlin); native camera APIs.
Flow: Capture → local temp → cloud (S3/GCS) → AI (blur/dupe/face sharpness) → user review → print manifest → lab API → tracking/notifications.
Back end: modular monolith or microservices (Auth, Photos/AI, Orders/Billing, Partner Integrations, Notifications). Queues/workers (SQS/Lambda/GKE). CDN for previews.
AI: start lightweight; clear user control and transparency; keep originals unless opted out.
Security/Privacy: TLS, scoped URLs, expiring tokens; no model training on user content by default; GDPR/CPRA; COPPA‑aware messaging.
Open‑source vs platform
Swift (language): open source (Apache 2.0).
iOS SDK: proprietary; WebKit/Swift open.
Kotlin: open source (Apache 2.0).
Android (AOSP): largely open; GMS/Play Services closed.
